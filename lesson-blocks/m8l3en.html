Personal Finance for International Professionals

You have a job at NASA, SpaceX, or a contractor. Congratulations. Now comes the financial architecture that determines if you build wealth or live paycheck to paycheck.

The US financial system is not designed for immigrants. Tax law assumes you are a US citizen. Visa rules add complexity. You need a clear financial strategy.

Building US Credit (3-6 Months)

  • Month 1: Open US bank account. Get debit card. Build history with one bank.
  • Month 2: Get secured credit card (Capital One or Discover IT). Deposit $500-$1,000 collateral. Charge $100/month, pay off immediately.
  • Month 3-4: Upgrade cards. Apply for second card. Build credit mix. Keep utilization below 30%.
  • Month 5-6: Monitor credit score. Check creditkarma.com or annualcreditreport.com. Target: 700+ by month 6-9.
  • Rewards: After 6 months on-time payments, upgrade to premium cards with airline miles and travel benefits.

Stock Options and RSUs: Understanding Your Equity

RSU: You get 1,000 RSUs vesting over 4 years (25% per year). On year 1 vesting: 250 RSUs convert to shares at fair market value. If stock is $200/share, you owe income tax on $50,000. Federal rate: 22-32%. You owe this tax even if you don't sell. Strategy: Sell some shares to cover the tax. Hold the rest for capital gains.

Stock Options (Startup): You get 10,000 options at $1 strike price. Company grows to $100. You exercise: buy 10,000 at $1, sell at $100. Profit: $990,000. Tax: Usually long-term capital gains (15% federal) if held properly.

Exercise window: When you leave, 90 days to exercise before expiration. Critical for startup founders.

Investment Basics: Index Funds, Asset Allocation, 4% Rule

Index funds vs. stocks: Index funds (VTSAX, VOO, VTI) track entire market. You own pieces of 3,000+ companies. Individual stocks are risky. Index funds outperform 90% of professional stock pickers.

Asset allocation by age: Age 25-35: 90% stocks, 10% bonds. Age 35-45: 80/20. Age 45-55: 70/30. Age 55-65: 60/40. Age 65+: 50/50.

The 4% rule: Withdraw 4% per year in retirement and it lasts 30+ years. $1M invested -> $40,000/year.

Dollar-cost averaging (DCA): Invest same amount every month ($500, $1,000). Buy more when prices low, less when high. Don't time the market.

Remittances Home: Platforms and Tax Implications

  • Wise: Mid-market exchange rate, low fees (0.5-2%). Best for large transfers.
  • Remitly: Fast (minutes to hours), slightly higher fees (2-4%). Good for small urgent transfers.
  • Bank wire: High fees ($20-$50), bad exchange rates. Avoid.

Tax note: Remittances are NOT deductible. You pay income tax, then send after-tax money home.

Capstone Quiz: Financial Literacy (7 Questions)

1. Hierarchy of retirement savings for maximum tax benefit?

B) Get employer 401(k) match, max HSA, max 401(k), then IRA

Answer: B. Priority: (1) Employer match (free money), (2) Max HSA (triple tax advantage), (3) Max 401(k), (4) Max IRA.
2. You earn $150K on H-1B, max 401(k) ($23,500). Tax savings?

B) $5,640

Answer: B. $23,500 x 24% (federal rate) = $5,640 federal savings. Add state taxes: $6,500-$7,500 total.
3. 1,000 RSUs, stock $200, vesting 250/year. Year 1 tax liability?

C) $12,000 federal plus state taxes

Answer: C. 250 shares x $200 = $50,000 taxable. At 24% federal: $12,000 owed, plus state taxes (~$2K-$4K). Total: $14K-$16K.
4. Send $5K monthly home. Most cost-effective platform?

B) Wise

Answer: B. Wise: 0.5-2% fees, mid-market rates. Saves you $250-$500/year vs. bank. Over 10 years: $2,500-$5,000.
5. The 4% rule: Invest $1M, withdraw annually?

B) $40,000

Answer: B. $1M x 4% = $40,000/year. Lasts 30+ years in retirement.
6. RSU vesting tax vs. long-term capital gains tax?

B) RSU vesting is ordinary income (22-37%). Post-vesting gains are long-term capital gains (15%).

Answer: B. Vesting day: ordinary income. Hold 1 year after vesting: long-term capital gains (15% federal).
7. Aracely's core message about legacy?

C) Build legacy as you go: mentor, invest in startups, create scholarships, share knowledge

Answer: C. Legacy happens simultaneously with wealth-building. Mentor early. Invest in LATAM startups. Build scholarships while building your own wealth.
